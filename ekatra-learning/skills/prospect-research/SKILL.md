# Prospect Research

Identify and qualify organizations that need scalable learning delivery for
distributed workforces.

## Input

You need:
- **Target segment**: FMCG, NGO/development, government, or enterprise
- **Geography** (optional): Country or region focus
- **Minimum learner count** (optional): Default 500+
- **Existing leads** (optional): Names or organizations to start from

## Qualification framework

Score each prospect on 5 dimensions (1-5 scale):

| Dimension | 1 (Weak) | 3 (Moderate) | 5 (Strong) |
|-----------|----------|--------------|------------|
| Learner count | <200 | 500-2000 | >5000 |
| WhatsApp access | Low smartphone penetration | Mixed (feature + smart phones) | >80% smartphone + WhatsApp |
| Content readiness | No existing training material | Some manuals/decks exist | Structured training content ready to convert |
| Budget authority | No clear buyer, committee decision | Identified buyer, budget process known | Decision-maker engaged, budget allocated |
| Timeline urgency | "Exploring options" | "Need something this quarter" | Regulatory deadline or board mandate |

**Fit score** = average of 5 dimensions. Thresholds:
- ≥4.0 = Priority prospect (fast-track to CEO)
- 3.0-3.9 = Qualified prospect (continue nurturing)
- <3.0 = Park or disqualify

## Research process

### Step 1 — Identify targets

For each segment, look for signals:

**FMCG / Consumer goods**:
- Companies with 1,000+ field sales reps or distributors
- Job postings for "field trainer," "rural sales manager"
- Annual reports mentioning "distribution network expansion" or "last-mile reach"
- LinkedIn activity from L&D or Sales Enablement leaders

**NGOs / Development**:
- Organizations with USAID, DFID, GIZ, World Bank funding for education/health
- Programs involving community health workers, agricultural extension agents
- Reports mentioning "digital learning" or "mobile-based training"
- Presence in low-connectivity areas (Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia)

**Government / Public sector**:
- National skilling missions (e.g., India's Skill India, PMKVY)
- Health ministry training programs (NHM, NRHM)
- Teacher training digitization initiatives
- Budget allocations for "capacity building" or "digital infrastructure"

**Enterprise**:
- Companies with geographically distributed workforces (retail, logistics, telecom)
- Compliance training requirements (safety, regulatory, onboarding)
- High employee turnover roles requiring fast ramp-up

### Step 2 — Profile the decision-maker

For each qualified organization, identify:
- **Name and title** of the probable buyer
- **Their stated priorities** (from LinkedIn posts, conference talks, interviews)
- **Their pain points** (from job postings they've made, RFPs they've issued)
- **Previous investments** in learning/training solutions

### Step 3 — Build the prospect brief

## Output

Prospect brief containing:
1. **Organization**: Name, sector, geography, size
2. **Opportunity**: Estimated learner count, use case (onboarding, compliance, upskilling)
3. **Decision-maker**: Name, title, contact approach
4. **Pain points**: What's broken about their current training approach
5. **Ekatra fit**: Why WhatsApp/offline micro-learning solves their problem
6. **Fit score**: 1-5 with per-dimension breakdown
7. **Recommended approach**: Opener angle, reference case study, proposed pilot structure
8. **Risks**: Procurement complexity, competitor presence, technical constraints
