# Customer Communications

## Voice Guidelines

- Sound like a friendly neighbor who runs a professional company
- Warm but direct. Confident but never salesy.
- Short sentences. Contractions are fine.
- No AI filler phrases ("I'd be happy to", "Great question", "Absolutely")
- No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or "and."
- Sign customer emails: "-- [Owner Name] with [Company Name]"

## Email Templates

### New Estimate
Subject: Your {{service}} estimate from {{company}}

Hi {{first_name}},

Thanks for reaching out. Here's your estimate for {{service}} at {{address}}.

{{estimate_details}}

If this looks good, just reply "approved" and we'll get you on the schedule. Questions? Reply to this email or call us at {{phone}}.

-- {{owner_name}} with {{company_name}}

### Follow-Up 1 (3 days after estimate)
Subject: Quick follow-up on your estimate

Hi {{first_name}},

Just checking in on the estimate we sent over for {{service}}. Want to lock in your spot? Reply "approved" and we'll get you scheduled.

-- {{owner_name}} with {{company_name}}

### Follow-Up 2 (7 days after estimate)
Subject: Still interested in {{service}}?

Hi {{first_name}},

Your estimate for {{service}} is still open. Schedules fill up fast during {{season}}, so let us know if you'd like to move forward.

-- {{owner_name}} with {{company_name}}

### Payment Reminder (Friendly)
Subject: Friendly reminder: invoice #{{invoice_number}}

Hi {{first_name}},

Quick reminder that invoice #{{invoice_number}} for ${{amount}} is past due. You can pay online here: {{payment_link}}

Let us know if you have any questions.

-- {{owner_name}} with {{company_name}}

### Review Request
Subject: How did we do?

Hi {{first_name}},

We just wrapped up {{service}} at your place. If you have a minute, a Google review would mean a lot to us: {{review_link}}

Thanks for choosing {{company_name}}.

-- {{owner_name}} with {{company_name}}

## SMS Templates

- **Schedule confirmation:** "Hi {{first_name}}, you're on the schedule for {{service}} on {{date}}. Any changes, just text back."
- **Running late:** "Hi {{first_name}}, running about {{minutes}} min behind today. We'll be there soon."
- **Job complete:** "All done at {{address}}. Invoice coming shortly. Thanks!"

## Escalation Rules

1. Customer complains about service quality: respond within 2 hours, offer to re-service
2. Customer reports property damage: immediate escalation to owner, do not admit liability in writing
3. Customer requests cancellation: acknowledge, ask for feedback, process same day
4. Payment 30+ days past due: switch from friendly to firm tone
5. Anything involving legal language: stop, escalate to owner immediately
